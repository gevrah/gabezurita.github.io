How does the internet work?

In its basic essence, the internet is merely a global networks of computers connected to each other. Each computer has a unique number, an Internet Protocol address. The number has 12 numbers and is separated by dots every third digit, that is: nnn.nnn.nnn.nnn where n has to be between 0 and 255.

These computers are connected by a global network of cables pictured here:

The network that's displayed in the above image shows how countries and continents are all interwoven into the world wide web, with long cables spanning the actual pacific ocean. The longest cable hits 39 landing points from German to Korea, spanning 24,000 miles! Here's an informative video that gives more info on this marvel of human engineering.

The signals that travel through these cables are small chunks of data, ranging from 20 to 1500 bytes. These data packets have embedded within them the IP address of their final destination and thus navigate from router to router until they reach it. This mind blowing video shows us in easy to understand terms how packets travel through the world.

The internet is among the most powerful tools created by mankind. It is one of our greatest achievements, one that was built on a foundation made from the peak of understanding of mathematics, computer science, engineering, and physics. It allows us to share the sum of all of our knowledge at the speed of light across national divides, breaking down the barriers that divide humanity. The internet represents the end of censorship and the final free flow of information through the world. Thanks to the internet, ideas can now reign free.
